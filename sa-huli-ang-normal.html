Sa Huli,
Ang Normal

( In The End, Normalcy )

The Characters

Dante: Wealthy, tailored, and hopelessly addicted to a love he cannot keep.

Nico: Rough around the edges, shirtless, and carrying the weight of a secret transaction.

Scene 01

The Safehouse

The room smells of stale cigarettes and cheap lavender air freshener. It is a contrast to the pristine, tailored suit Dante just carelessly threw onto the dirty carpet.

Dante stands by the window, watching the heavy Manila rain blur the city lights. Behind him, the sound of a zipper breaking the silence makes his shoulders tense up.

"Haharap ka ba o tatayo ka na lang diyan?" Nico’s voice is rough, dripping with a mix of exhaustion and hunger.

Dante turns around. Nico is sitting on the edge of the bed, shirtless, his tattoos flexing as he leans back on his elbows. He looks like trouble—the kind Dante’s family warned him about, the kind Dante has been addicted to for five years.

"Wala tayong oras para mag-inarte, Dante," Nico adds, his eyes dark and heavy. "Last na 'to. Make it count."

"Don’t tell me what to do. Alam ko ang ginagawa ko," Dante strides forward, grabbing Nico by the jaw.

Consumption

Scene 02

The kiss is violent. It isn’t sweet; it tastes like rust and desperation. Dante pushes Nico onto the mattress, the springs creaking loudly in the small room.

"F***... Ang bango mo. Amoy mayaman ka na naman," Nico curses against Dante’s mouth, his hands tangling into Dante’s expensive hair.

"Shut up," Dante growls, biting down on Nico’s lower lip until he tastes copper.

He moves his hand down, his touch possessive, claiming territory he knows he’s about to lose. Nico arches his back, letting out a fractured moan.

"D*** it, Dante... dahan-dahan naman, g*go."

"Sabi mo, make it count?" Dante whispers hoarsely into Nico’s ear. He pins Nico’s wrists above his head. "Ipararamdam ko sa'yo kung ano ang sinasayang mo."

The friction is intense, a chaotic rhythm of skin slapping against skin. There is no romance here, only the raw, animalistic need to memorize a body.

"Ah! F***... s***... Diyan... h'wag kang titigil... p*tangina..." Nico gasps, his nails digging into Dante’s shoulders.

Dante moves with a punishing pace, sweat dripping from his forehead onto Nico’s chest. Every thrust is an accusation. Why are you doing this? Why are we ending?

"Say my name."

"Dante... ah! Dante... Sa'yo ako... f***... sa'yo lang ako ngayon."

For a few minutes, the world outside—the obligations, the lies—doesn't exist. There is only the heat, the wet sounds of their bodies colliding, and the vulgar, honest prayers they scream into the empty air.

Scene 03

The Cold Air

The silence after is deafening. Dante is buttoning his shirt, his fingers trembling slightly. Nico is smoking by the window, blowing smoke into the rain. They are back to being strangers sharing a room.

"Inayos ko na ang cheque," Dante says, his voice devoid of emotion. He places an envelope on the bedside table. "Sapat na 'yan para sa puhunan na kailangan mo sa probinsya."

Nico doesn’t look at the envelope. He just nods. "Salamat."

"Masaya ka na?" Dante sneers, fixing his tie in the mirror. "Makakatakas ka na sa magulong buhay dito sa Maynila. You get your fresh start."

"Hindi ito tungkol sa saya, Dante. Alam mo 'yan," Nico flicks the cigarette ash.

"Oo nga pala. It's about doing the 'right thing.' Being a good son. A good man."

Nico turns to him, his eyes sad but firm.

"Pagod na ako, Dante. Pagod na akong magtago. Pagod na akong maging sikreto mo. Gusto ko ng buhay na... tahimik. Yung hindi ko kailangang lumingon sa likod bawat hawak ko sa kamay ng kasama ko."

Dante stops. That hurts more than the breakup.

"Aalis na ako. Good luck sa 'normal' na buhay mo."

The Reality

Scene 04

Nico watches the door close. He waits for the sound of Dante’s car to fade away before he moves. He walks over to the table, but he doesn't pick up the envelope with the money. Instead, he picks up his phone which has been buzzing incessantly.

He swipes to answer.

"Hello, Ma?" Nico answers, his voice shifting to a gentle, respectful tone.

"Nak, nasaan ka na? Andito na yung fiancé ng kapatid mo. Hinihintay ka na namin para sa final fitting ng barong."

"Pauwi na ako, Ma. Traffic lang," Nico sighs, rubbing his temples.

"Bilisan mo. Alam mo naman, malaking utang na loob natin ito kay Dante. Kung hindi dahil sa kanya, hindi ma-ooperahan ang Papa mo. At hindi rin makaka-ahon ang kapatid mo. Napakabait na tao, 'no? Swerte ng ate mo sa kanya."

Nico freezes. He looks at the unmade bed where the scent of Dante still lingers.

"Opo, Ma," Nico whispers, his voice trembling. "Swerte ni Ate... kay Dante."

The Truth

Nico isn't leaving for the province to start a business. Nico is the future brother-in-law.

The "harsh truth" isn't that Dante is marrying someone else. It's that Nico brokered the marriage. Nico introduced his own sister to Dante months ago. Nico manipulated the situation because his family needed the medical financial support that only a connection to Dante’s family could provide.

Nico sacrificed his relationship, pushing the love of his life to marry his own sister, trading their romance for his family's survival.

The "normal life" Nico wanted wasn't for himself—it was stability for his parents and sister, paid for by selling Dante into a loveless marriage.

Nico picks up the envelope Dante left. It’s not "capital" for a business. It’s the payment for the wedding catering Nico organized. He keeps the money, turns off the lights, and walks out into the rain to attend his ex-lover's wedding rehearsal dinner.

THE END
