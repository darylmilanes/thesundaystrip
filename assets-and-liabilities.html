( An Hostile Takeover )

Deal Profiles

Jace: The "Rich." An heir to a failing shipping empire. Arrogant, desperate, and believes his charm can solve his financial crisis.

Vance: The "Richer." A mysterious venture capitalist with old money roots and a reputation for eating companies alive. Cold, calculating, and dangerously attractive.

Scene 01

The Valuation

The air in the private VIP lounge of the high-rise BGC hotel was thick with the scent of expensive scotch and unspoken agendas. Jace adjusted his silk tie, his heart pounding against his ribs, though his face remained a mask of practiced boredom.

Nakatayo si Vance by the floor-to-ceiling window, hawak ang isang crystal glass. Even from the back, the man exuded power—yung klase ng yaman na hindi na kailangang sumigaw.

"You’re late," Vance said, his voice deep and rough like gravel.

Humarap siya, and his gaze raked over Jace lazily. "I don't like waiting, Jace."

"I’m worth the wait, Vance," Jace replied, forcing a smirk as he walked closer. "Besides, I brought the merger proposal."

Vance chuckled darkly, placing his drink on the table. He didn't look at the documents. Instead, he stepped into Jace’s personal space. Masyadong malapit. Amoy ni Jace ang mamahaling pabango nito—a mix of sandalwood and something darker, musky.

"I didn't come here for papers, Jace. Alam ng lahat sa business circle na your family’s company is bleeding cash. You’re desperate."

Hinawakan ni Vance ang baba ni Jace, forcing him to look up. The touch was electric, sending a shiver down Jace’s spine.

"I can save you," Vance murmured, his thumb brushing against Jace’s lower lip. "Pero alam mo na hindi pera ang kabayaran ko."

Jace’s breath hitched. Alam niya ang laro. He stepped closer, eliminating the gap between their bodies.

"Then tell me... what’s the price?"

Due Diligence

Scene 02

Setting: Vance’s penthouse suite. The lights were dim, city lights serving as the only illumination. The merger papers lay forgotten on the floor.

Vance had Jace pinned against the mahogany desk. Their breaths came in ragged gasps. Hands were roaming, desperate and possessive. Vance’s grip on Jace’s waist was firm, demanding total submission.

"You think you can manipulate me with this?" Vance whispered against Jace’s neck, his lips grazing the sensitive skin below the ear. "Akala mo ba hindi ko alam ang ginagawa mo?"

"Shut up and just sign the deal," Jace panted.

Jace tilted his head back as Vance’s hand slid under his shirt, exploring the warmth of his skin. Mainit ang palad ni Vance, madiin ang bawat haplos. It felt like branding.

Vance bit lightly on Jace’s collarbone, eliciting a low moan from the heir. "You’re beautiful when you’re desperate, Jace. Sobrang ganda."

Inangat ni Vance si Jace paupo sa mesa, sweeping the expensive ornaments aside with a crash. Neither cared. Jace wrapped his legs around Vance’s waist, pulling him closer, seeking friction. The heat between them was suffocating, intoxicating.

"Make me yours, Vance," Jace challenged, his eyes heavy with lust. "Show me what real power feels like."

"Be careful what you wish for."

Vance captured Jace’s lips in a searing, dominant kiss that tasted of whiskey and danger.

Scene 03

The Acquisition

The bedroom was quiet, save for the sound of heavy breathing and the rustle of silk sheets. The atmosphere was heavy with the aftermath of their intensity. It was raw, suggestive, and deeply intimate.

Nakahiga si Jace, his chest heaving, glistening with sweat. Vance hovered over him, tracing the line of Jace’s jaw with a deceptive gentleness. The intimacy felt real—too real. For a moment, Jace forgot about the bankruptcy, the lies, and the scheme.

"You’re shaking," puna ni Vance, his voice softer now.

"Pagod lang," Jace lied, turning his face away.

He felt vulnerable. Exposed. He had given his body to secure his legacy. Vance leaned down, whispering directly into Jace’s ear, his voice sending shivers through Jace’s entire being.

"Ang sarap mong angkinin, Jace. Not just for a night. But completely."

Jace looked back at him, confusion warring with the haze of pleasure. "Once you sign the papers tomorrow, we’re partners. Equal footing."

Vance smiled—a slow, predatory smile that didn't reach his eyes. "Of course. Tomorrow."

He kissed Jace’s forehead, then his nose, before claiming his lips again, deeper this time, parang tinatatakan ang kaluluwa ni Jace. It was a kiss of finality, though Jace didn't know it yet.

The Liquidation

Scene 04

Morning came with a blinding glare. Jace woke up alone in the massive bed. His body ached pleasantly, a reminder of the night’s intensity. He sat up, expecting to see the signed merger agreement on the nightstand. Instead, he saw a single black folder with the emblem of Vance’s holding company.

Confident that he had won, Jace opened the folder while sipping the coffee left for him. Binasa niya ang unang pahina. His smile faded. Binasa niya ang ikalawa. His hands started to tremble. By the third page, the coffee cup fell from his hand, shattering on the floor.

Deed of Absolute Sale

This Instrument, made and entered into... confirms the complete transfer of all assets, properties, and holdings of Jace Shipping Corp to Vance Holdings...

Date: 3 Months Ago

Before Jace could process it, Vance walked in, fully dressed in a sharp charcoal suit, looking immaculate. He leaned against the doorframe, watching Jace with cold amusement.

"Binili mo ang kumpanya ko?" Jace’s voice broke. "But... we were negotiating last night. Why did you... why did you sleep with me if you already owned everything?"

Vance walked over slowly, looming over the bed. "Correction, Jace. I didn't just buy your company three months ago. I bought your family’s debt. I bought your properties."

May kinuha si Vance sa bulsa niya—isang maliit na litrato. It was an old, crumpled photo of a young boy crying outside a gated mansion—Jace's mansion.

"Do you remember the driver your father framed for theft fifteen years ago? The man who died in prison because your family needed a scapegoat? That was my father."

Jace froze. The color drained from his face.

"I didn't become rich to merge with you, Jace. I became rich to buy you."

Jace scrambled back against the headboard. "So... last night...?"

"Last night wasn't a negotiation," Vance said, leaning in close, trapping Jace again. "It was an appraisal."

The Contract

Vance tossed a new document onto Jace’s lap.

"You have nothing left. No home, no money, no name. Except this."

ROLE: Personal Assistant to the CEO

TERM: Indefinite

BENEFITS: Room and Board Included.

"Wala kang pupuntahan, Jace. You are destitute," Vance whispered, brushing a stray hair from Jace’s trembling face. "Unless you sign that. You wanted to be taken care of? I’ll take care of you. Forever."

Jace looked at the door, then at Vance. He realized with horror that despite the betrayal, despite the trap... part of him didn't want to leave. The mystery wasn't how Vance got so rich. The mystery was why Jace, staring at the man who ruined him, felt a twisted sense of relief.

"Sign it," Vance commanded softly.

With a shaking hand, Jace picked up the pen.
